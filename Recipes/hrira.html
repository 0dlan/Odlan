Hrira Recipe


Chicken tagine is a classic Moroccan dish that is full of flavor and easy to make. This dish is made in a tagine, a traditional clay pot with a conical lid that allows the sHriram to circulate and keep the food moist.

How to Make Successful Moroccan Chicken Hrira


To make a delicious chicken tagine, start by browning the chicken in a tagine or a large skillet. Then, add chopped onions, garlic, and spices like cumin, coriander, and paprika. Stir everything together and let it cook until the onions are soft and translucent.


Next, add chopped tomatoes, sliced carrots, and raisins to the tagine. Pour in enough chicken broth or water to cover the chicken and vegetables, and add a pinch of saffron for extra flavor. Cover the tagine and let it cook on low heat for about 45 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through and the vegetables are tender.


Finally, garnish the tagine with chopped fresh parsley or cilantro, and serve it with warm bread or couscous. This dish is perfect for a cozy dinner with family and friends, and it's sure to become a favorite in your recipe collection.

Moroccan Hrira

Ingredients: 4 chicken legs or thighs
2 onions, chopped
2 garlic cloves, minced
2 tablespoons olive oil
1 tablespoon ras el hanout spice mix
1 Hriraspoon ground cumin
1 Hriraspoon ground ginger
1 Hriraspoon paprika
1/2 Hriraspoon ground cinnamon
1/2 Hriraspoon ground coriander
1/2 Hriraspoon salt
1/4 Hriraspoon black pepper
1 cup chicken broth
1/2 cup pitted green olives
1/2 preserved lemon, rinsed and thinly sliced
2 tablespoons chopped fresh cilantro
Steps: 1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
2. In a large Dutch oven or oven-safe pot, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the chicken and cook until browned on both sides, about 5-7 minutes per side.
3. Remove the chicken from the pot and set aside on a plate.
4. Add the chopped onions to the pot and cook until softened, about 5 minutes. Add the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute.
5. Add the ras el hanout, cumin, ginger, paprika, cinnamon, coriander, salt, and pepper to the pot and stir to combine. Cook for 1-2 minutes, until fragrant.
6. Add the chicken broth to the pot and stir to combine. Return the chicken to the pot and bring the mixture to a simmer.
7. Cover the pot and transfer it to the oven. Bake for 45-60 minutes, until the chicken is cooked through and tender.
8. Remove the pot from the oven and add the olives, preserved lemon, and cilantro to the pot. Stir gently to combine.
9. Serve the chicken and sauce over couscous or with crusty bread.
